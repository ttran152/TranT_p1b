Design Principles

Clarity

Eliminate ambiguity. Enable people to see, understand, and act with confidence.

Efficiency

Streamline and optimize workflows. Intelligently anticipate needs to help people work better, smarter, and faster.

Consistency

Create familiarity and strengthen intuition by applying the same solution to the same problem.

Beauty

Demonstrate respect for people’s time and attention through thoughtful and elegant craftsmanship.

LOGO

1. Design

  • The logo is made up of six circles — they come together to create a cloud, which represents the origin of Salesforce.
  • Font: Salesforce Sans (created for Salesforce only)
  • Color: R13 G157 B218 / Web #0d9dda / C85 M19 Y0 K0 / Pantone 299

2. General rules

  • Follow the logo guidelines to protect the symbol of the brand
  • All product teams should use the primary logo, not a logo lockup
  • Logo should always be used in Salesforce Blue.

Colors

1. Primary Colors

The iconic Salesforce Blue is the star of the corporate color palette, which draws inspiration from day and night skies. It’s also a subtle nod to their pioneering spirit in cloud computing.

2. Secondary Colors

A natural, earthy, organically- inspired secondary palette serves as a base for their designs. Think lighter neutrals for backgrounds, and for the text, the darker shades work best for accessibility.

3. Product Colors

Each product color draws inspiration from nature and is represented throughout their brand in marketing, product, and real estate.

Typography

The paragraph typeface of Salesforce is Salesforce Sans, a font that guarantee legibility and is made just for Salesforce. They also combine with an “old yet new” companion as a heading, Avant Garde, a 1970’s classic.

Illustration

Salesforce has a set of “Characters” that involve in many different designs. These characters reflect their human side, and differentiate the company from our competitors as a fun brand. These characters deliver the company communications, guide you through your career goals, and help you stay on track. On the outside, they represent community groups such as our developers, admins, architects, and Trailblazers among others. And to our customers, which vary from newly signed prospects to those already in the fold, they make our messages more relevant and memorable.

The illustration style is partially inspired by the classic Work Projects Administration (WPA) posters depicting U.S. national parks. These colorful illustration shows the whimsical and fun side of our company and culture. It helps set our brand apart, broadens brand awareness, and creates friendly and positive feelings about Salesforce. The characters are usually seen in the nature, exploring and getting ready for a new challenge.

Photography

While the photos aren’t heavily filtered, the real people are surrounded by icons, characters and illustration to demonstrate the exciting, innovative products and services provided by Salesforce. These people look like they are joining Salesforce’s own world with their lovely characters. Since there are a lot of illustration, there are only 1-3 people in a design.

Iconography

a) Utility Icon

Utility icons are simple, single- color glyphs that identify labels and actions across form factors

b) Doctype Icon

Doctype icons represent document file formats and are full colors

c) Standard & custom icons

Standard Object and Custom Object icons represent Salesforce entities and objects (e.g., Accounts, Leads, Cases, etc.)

d) Action Icon

Action icons are for use with quick actions on touch-screen devices
